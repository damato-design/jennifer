I'm a design systems and product designer who turns scattered, tangled, "we-can-figure-it-out-later" problems into scalable, accessible solutions. And yes, I make it look easy. _(Most of the time.)_

My design journey started with hands-on creativity and found its footing in the digital world. Along the way, I've become someone with strong opinions, loosely held. That last part matters. It means I come in with a point of view, and I leave room for yours.

I believe great design isn't about finding the one "right" answer. It's about exploring the ideas that actually spark something, then building them in a way that works for everyone, including the people who use screen readers, tab through interfaces, and notice when contrast ratio is off by 0.1. _(I notice. I always notice.)_

When I'm not knee-deep in component libraries or nudging a color to pass contrast, you'll find me on a climbing wall at the gym, stomping through a DDR set _(yes, that's my cardio, and of course, I take it seriously)_, taste-testing my way through new restaurants, experimenting in the kitchen, or lost in a game of Random Dice.

[Let's chat](https://cal.com/jennifer-damato). Tell me about your latest project, your favorite restaurant, or the design system problem that's been quietly haunting you. I'd genuinely love to hear it.
